b) Determination of Calcium Oxide (CaO)

Section Overview:

This section focuses on the quantitative determination of calcium oxide (CaO) content in the cement sample. The process involves making the filtrate alkaline, precipitating interfering ions, and then using complexometric titration or other analytical methods to determine the calcium content accurately.

Key Chemical Principle: Calcium ions are selectively determined after removing interfering metal ions through controlled pH adjustments and specific precipitation reactions.

Step 1: Adding Ammonium Hydroxide

Add NH₄OH dropwise to the filtrate until the solution becomes slightly alkaline (pH ~8-9). This step removes interfering metal hydroxides.
